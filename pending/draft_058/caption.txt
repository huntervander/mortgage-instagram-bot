Are you paying for mortgage insurance you don't need? 💡

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is one of those costs that catches a lot of first-time buyers off guard. Here's what you need to know so you can keep more money in your pocket.

📌 What is PMI?
PMI is an extra monthly fee added to your conventional loan payment when your down payment is less than 20%. It protects the lender — not you — if you were to default.

📌 What does it actually cost?
Typically 0.5%–1.5% of your loan amount per year. On a $350,000 loan, that's roughly $146–$438 per month on top of your regular payment.

📌 How do you get rid of it?
Once you've built 20% equity in your home, you can request PMI removal. By law, your lender must cancel it automatically when you hit 22% equity — based on your original purchase price.

📌 VA Loan advantage: If you're a veteran or active-duty service member, VA loans have NO PMI — ever. That's one of the biggest financial benefits of the program, and as a veteran-owned business we love helping veterans take advantage of it.
