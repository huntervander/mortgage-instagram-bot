What is PMI — and do you actually have to pay it? 🏡

If you're buying a home with less than 20% down on a conventional loan, your lender will likely require Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). Here's what you need to know:

🔹 PMI protects the lender — not you. If you default, the insurance pays the lender. You pay the premium.

🔹 The cost varies, but PMI typically adds somewhere between 0.5%–1.5% of your loan amount per year to your monthly payment. On a $300,000 loan, that could be $125–$375/month extra.

🔹 The good news: PMI isn't forever. On conventional loans, you can request cancellation once you reach 20% equity, and it's required to be removed automatically at 22%.

🔹 Ways to avoid PMI:
  • Put 20% down
  • Use a VA loan (no PMI ever — one of the biggest VA benefits!)
  • Ask about lender-paid PMI options

Understanding PMI upfront helps you plan your purchase strategy and avoid surprises on closing day.
