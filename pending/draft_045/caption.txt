What is PMI — and do you actually need to pay it? 🏡

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is a monthly fee added to most conventional loans when you put down less than 20%. Here's the thing: it protects your lender, not you — but you're the one paying for it.

Here's what you should know:
• Once you reach 20% equity in your home, you can request PMI removal
• VA loans have NO PMI — one of the biggest benefits for eligible veterans & active duty
• FHA loans have their own mortgage insurance (MIP), which works a bit differently
• Some loan strategies and programs can help you avoid or minimize PMI from the start

The right loan program can make a significant difference in your monthly payment. It all starts with knowing your options.
