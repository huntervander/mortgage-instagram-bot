Your credit score could be the difference between your dream home and a missed opportunity. 🏠

Your credit score is one of the biggest factors lenders use to determine your loan eligibility and interest rate. Even a small difference in your score can translate to thousands of dollars saved (or spent) over the life of your loan.

Here's how scores generally map to your home loan options:
• 740+: Typically qualifies for the best conventional loan rate tiers
• 680–739: Strong — great conventional and FHA options available
• 620–679: FHA loans are designed to help buyers in this range
• Below 620: Don't count yourself out — there may still be paths forward. Let's talk.

Three things you can do right now to strengthen your score:
✅ Pay down revolving balances — aim to stay below 30% of each card's credit limit
✅ Hold off on opening new accounts before you apply — new inquiries can temporarily lower your score
✅ Pull your free report at AnnualCreditReport.com and dispute any errors you find

The sooner you start, the better positioned you'll be when you're ready to buy or refinance.
