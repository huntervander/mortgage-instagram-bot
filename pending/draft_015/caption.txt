Your credit score gets all the attention — but your debt-to-income ratio (DTI) is just as important when qualifying for a mortgage. 📊

What is DTI?
DTI is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward monthly debt payments. Lenders use it to measure how much financial room you have.

How it's calculated:
👉 Add up all monthly debt payments (car, student loans, credit cards, other loans)
👉 Divide by your gross monthly income (before taxes)
👉 That percentage = your DTI

General guidelines:
✅ Below 36% — excellent
✅ 36–43% — generally acceptable for most programs
✅ 43–50% — may still qualify depending on loan type, but tighter
⚠️ Above 50% — difficult to qualify with most programs

VA loans can offer more flexibility on DTI than conventional loans in some cases — one more advantage for eligible veterans.

How to improve DTI before applying: pay down existing debt, or increase your income. Even eliminating a small monthly payment can move the needle.
