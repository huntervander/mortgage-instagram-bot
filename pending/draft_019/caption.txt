The purchase price isn't the only number you need to budget for. Closing costs catch a lot of buyers off guard — here's what they actually are. 💼🏡

What are closing costs?
Fees and charges due at closing, separate from your down payment.

What's typically included:
📋 Origination and lender fees
🏠 Appraisal fee
📝 Title insurance and settlement fees
💸 Prepaid items (homeowners insurance, property tax escrow, prepaid interest)
🏛️ Government recording fees

How much to expect:
Generally 2–5% of the loan amount. On a $350,000 loan, that's $7,000–$17,500.

🎖️ VA loan advantage at closing:
Sellers can contribute up to 4% of the loan in concessions, and the VA limits what lenders can charge veterans — making VA one of the most cost-efficient options at closing. Some veterans close with very little out of pocket.

Can closing costs be rolled in or covered?
In some cases yes — through seller concessions, lender credits, or programs like Florida Hometown Heroes. Ask us what applies to your situation.
