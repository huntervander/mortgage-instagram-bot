Do you know your Debt-to-Income Ratio — and why it matters? 📊

Your DTI is one of the first numbers a lender looks at when you apply for a home loan. Here's how it works:

📌 What is DTI?
It's the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward monthly debt payments — car loans, student loans, credit cards, and your future mortgage payment combined.

📌 General benchmarks to know:
• Conventional loans: typically prefer a DTI at or below 45%
• FHA loans: may allow up to 57% in certain situations
• VA loans (for eligible veterans): flexible, but lower is always better

📌 How to lower your DTI before applying:
✅ Pay down revolving balances (credit cards first)
✅ Avoid taking on new debt before closing
✅ Hold off on big purchases (car, furniture) until after you close

A lower DTI = more loan options and stronger purchasing power. Worth knowing before you start your home search!
