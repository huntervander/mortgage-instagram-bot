Do you know what PMI is — and how it affects your monthly payment? 🏠

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is a fee lenders require when your down payment is less than 20% on a conventional loan. It protects the lender — not you — but you're the one paying for it.

Here's what every homebuyer should know:
📌 PMI typically adds 0.5%–1.5% of your loan amount per year to your payment
📌 It's not permanent — PMI goes away once you reach 20% equity in your home
📌 Some loan types handle mortgage insurance differently (FHA has its own MIP structure)

Ways to reduce or avoid PMI:
✅ Put 20% down on a conventional loan
✅ Ask about FHA loans if you have a lower credit score or smaller down payment
✅ If you're an eligible veteran — VA loans have NO PMI, ever
✅ Florida Hometown Heroes and other down payment assistance programs can help you reach that 20% faster
