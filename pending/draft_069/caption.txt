🏠 What is your Debt-to-Income Ratio — and why does every lender care about it?

If you've started researching home loans, you've probably come across the term DTI (Debt-to-Income Ratio). It's one of the most important numbers lenders look at, and understanding it can help you prepare long before you apply.

**What is DTI?**
Your DTI is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward all your monthly debt obligations — including your future mortgage payment, car loans, student loans, and credit card minimums.

**How it's calculated:**
Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income = DTI %
Example: $1,800 in debts ÷ $6,000 income = 30% DTI

**General guidelines by loan type:**
• Conventional: Lenders typically prefer 45% or lower
• FHA: May allow higher DTI with strong compensating factors
• VA Loans: No hard cap, but lower DTI always strengthens your file

**Quick ways to improve your DTI:**
✅ Pay down revolving debt (especially credit cards)
✅ Avoid taking on new loans before you apply
✅ Consider ways to increase your qualifying income

The lower your DTI, the stronger your application — and often, the better your rate options.
