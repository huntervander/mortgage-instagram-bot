APR vs. interest rate — what's the actual difference? 🤔

These two numbers show up on every loan estimate, and they're not the same thing. Knowing the difference can save you from comparing apples to oranges when you're shopping lenders.

📌 Interest rate is the cost you pay annually to borrow the loan amount — this is what determines your monthly principal and interest payment.

📌 APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the broader number — it wraps in the interest rate plus most lender fees and closing costs, expressed as a yearly percentage. APR is meant to give you a fuller picture of the loan's true cost over time.

📌 Why it matters: two loans with the same interest rate can have very different APRs depending on fees. When comparing offers, look at both — a lower rate with sky-high fees can end up costing more.
