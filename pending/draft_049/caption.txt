APR vs. Interest Rate — what's the difference? 🏡

These two numbers appear on every Loan Estimate, and they are NOT the same thing. Here's a quick breakdown:

📌 Interest Rate — the annual cost of borrowing the loan principal. This is what directly sets your monthly payment amount.

📌 APR (Annual Percentage Rate) — the interest rate PLUS most loan fees rolled in (origination fees, mortgage insurance, discount points, etc.), expressed as a yearly percentage. It reflects the true total cost of borrowing over the life of the loan.

👉 This is why your APR is almost always higher than your stated interest rate.

When you're comparing loan offers from different lenders, compare APRs — not just interest rates. It levels the playing field and helps you spot the better deal.
