Did you know your credit score can save — or cost — you thousands on your mortgage? 🏠

Your credit score is one of the biggest factors lenders look at when you apply for a home loan. Even a 20-point difference can shift your interest rate — and that adds up fast over a 30-year loan.

Here's a quick look at how scores affect your loan options:
• 760+ → Best available rates, widest choice of loan programs
• 700–759 → Competitive rates, great options across conventional & FHA
• 640–699 → FHA and select conventional loans accessible
• 580–639 → FHA loans with 3.5% down may be available
• Below 580 → More limited options; worth building your score first

The good news? Credit scores can be improved. Paying down credit card balances, avoiding new inquiries in the months before you apply, and correcting errors on your credit report can all move the needle.
