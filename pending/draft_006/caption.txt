Ever notice your loan estimate shows TWO different percentages? Here's the difference. 📊

🔢 Interest Rate — the cost of borrowing the loan amount itself. This is the number used to calculate your monthly principal & interest payment.

🔢 APR (Annual Percentage Rate) — your interest rate PLUS certain lender fees and closing costs, rolled into one yearly percentage. It reflects the bigger-picture cost of the loan.

Why it matters:
✅ A loan with a slightly higher rate but lower fees could have a LOWER APR than one with a flashy low rate and high fees
✅ Comparing APRs (not just rates) between lenders gives you a more apples-to-apples comparison
✅ Your actual rate and APR depend on your credit, loan type, and the market on the day you lock
