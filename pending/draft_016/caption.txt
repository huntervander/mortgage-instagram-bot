"Should I lock my rate now or wait?" It's one of the most common questions we get — and the honest answer might surprise you. 🔒📊

Here's the reality of rate locking:

🔍 What a rate lock does: It freezes your interest rate for a set period (typically 30–60 days) while your loan processes. If rates rise during that time, you're protected. If they drop, you generally don't benefit — unless you have a float-down option built in.

📉 Can you time the market? Rarely. Even professional economists can't reliably predict short-term rate movements. Rates react to jobs data, inflation reports, Fed signals, and global events — often within hours of a release.

🎯 The practical approach: Lock when the rate works for YOUR budget and monthly payment goals — not because you think rates will drop later. A rate that works for you today is more valuable than waiting for one that may never arrive.

🎖️ VA borrowers: Your no-PMI benefit already puts your break-even payment ahead of most conventional borrowers at the same rate. When the numbers pencil out, lock with confidence.
