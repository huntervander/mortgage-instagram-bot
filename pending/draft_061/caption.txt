What is PMI — and can you actually avoid it? 🏡

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is a monthly fee added to most conventional loans when you put less than 20% down. Here's the thing: it protects the lender, not you — but you're the one paying for it.

How much does it cost? Typically 0.5%–1.5% of your loan amount per year. On a $300,000 loan, that can add up to a significant chunk of your monthly payment.

The good news — PMI isn't permanent on a conventional loan:
• You can request removal once you hit 20% equity
• It cancels automatically when you reach 22% equity

Want to skip PMI altogether? Here are your best options:
✅ Put 20%+ down on a conventional loan
✅ Look into a VA loan — eligible veterans and active-duty service members get 0% down AND no PMI, ever
