The Fed gets talked about constantly when mortgage rates move — but most people don't know what the connection actually is. Let's clear it up. 📊🏦

First: The Fed does NOT directly set mortgage rates.

The Federal Reserve controls the federal funds rate — the overnight lending rate between banks. Mortgage rates are primarily driven by the 10-year Treasury yield and mortgage-backed securities (MBS) markets, which react to economic data like inflation, jobs numbers, and yes, Fed signals.

How they connect:
📌 When the Fed signals higher rates for longer → bond yields tend to rise → mortgage rates often follow
📌 When the economy softens or inflation cools → bond investors buy Treasuries → yields drop → mortgage rates can fall

What this means right now:
✅ Rate movement can happen fast — sometimes within hours of a data release
✅ Waiting for the Fed to "cut rates" doesn't guarantee mortgage relief — markets often price cuts in before they happen
✅ The best strategy: control what you can (credit score, debt, savings) and lock when YOUR numbers work
